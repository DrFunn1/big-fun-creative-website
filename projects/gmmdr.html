Giant Marble Machine - Drag Race

Description

The Giant Marble Machine - Drag Race combines two universally beloved spectacles: marble machines and drag racing. This 24-foot tall kinetic sculpture captivates audiences as massive one-foot diameter marbles (upcycled 1970s driveway light covers) are lifted by an impressive elevator system to the starting gate. Spectators gather to place friendly bets while sensors detect when two marbles are ready to race, triggering a "Christmas tree" countdown sequence. The 45-foot drag strip culminates in a photo-finish flag and secure catch box that returns the marbles safely to the elevator base.

This ambitious installation presented significant fabrication challenges—no one on the team had previously built a giant marble machine. Through iterative problem-solving and learning from each setback, the team developed a robust system capable of running over 500 races before requiring maintenance. Working within a modest budget for its 40×40×24 foot footprint, the design maximizes readily available materials: dimensional lumber, plywood, electrical conduit, laser-cut luan track supports, and cabinet screws.

The collaborative build process divided the team into specialized groups tackling distinct sections: the elevator lift mechanism, start box, Christmas tree lights, raceway, finish box, flying spiral, musical shelves, and slalom finale. The electronics team integrated control systems for the AC motor, starting gate, countdown sequence, finish flag, and ball return gating. All controls were consolidated at eye level behind transparent covers, allowing curious visitors to observe the Arduino microcontrollers and variable frequency drive that orchestrate the spectacle—turning the control system itself into an educational exhibit.

Technical Details

  • Dimensions: 6x24x50 foot for the streamlined final version
  • Materials: Wood, steel, electrical conduit, pex piping, hdpe hollow light covers
  • Interactivity: Press the go button, place your bets!
  • Technology: Three Arduino microcontrollers, industrial leaf sensors, variable freqency motor speed controller
  • Fabrication Time: 16 months
  • Budget: $5,000

Placements

Status

Currently Installed
